MIDHEAVEN HAS PUT UP THEIR LIMITED CD EDITION OF THE ALBUM 'NOT THAT FAR AWAY' TO STOCK

FEB 25, 2026


Emerging from the heart of the independent scene, Midheaven is set to release a limited edition CD of their acclaimed album Not That Far Away Up to Stock. This special physical pressing brings a tangible chapter to the band’s evolving sound, offering fans a collectible experience beyond digital streaming.

After gaining traction with their unique blend of atmospheric rock and introspective songwriting, Midheaven has built a dedicated following eager for physical media. The new CD edition answers that demand with a release that’s as thoughtfully crafted as the music it holds.

What Makes This Edition Special


The limited CD version isn’t just a standard press — it’s designed as a piece of art:

〇 Premium packaging with exclusive artwork and band imagery

〇 Hand-numbered copies, ensuring authenticity and collectibility

〇 Lyrics and liner notes included, giving deeper insight into the album’s themes

〇 A curated visual presentation that complements the sonic landscape of Not That Far Away Up to Stock

These features make it a must-have for collectors and long-time supporters of the band.

A Milestone for Midheaven


The limited CD version isn’t just a standard press — it’s designed as a piece of art:

Not That Far Away Up to Stock originally captured listeners with its richly textured arrangements and reflective lyrical journey. Tracks like “Stairs You Have To Climb” and “Shards of Glass” helped establish Midheaven as a band capable of balancing raw emotion with refined musicianship.

The decision to launch a limited physical edition underlines Midheaven’s commitment to giving fans something beyond the invisible playlists — a tactile, shareable piece of music culture.

How to Order


Copies of the limited CD will be available while supplies last. Given the restricted run, fans are encouraged to secure theirs early. More details on pricing and distribution will be updated on both Midheaven’s official channels and here on Stredneba Records.
